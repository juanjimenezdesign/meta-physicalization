Governance · Ethics

Ethical commitments

How this tool aligns with Carleton's AI Framework, Ontario's Bill 194, and FIPPA — and the pedagogical principles that ground every AI-assisted action.

Carleton's AI Framework (March 2026)

Responsible & ethical use. Human agency and transparency. AI is a supplementary technology and never replaces human decision-making. Final evaluation of students' work is always done by the course instructor, especially when AI assists with grading.

Ontario Bill 194 & FIPPA

Strengthening Cybersecurity and Building Trust in the Public Sector Act (2024) and the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act shape how this tool handles data. All audience responses are anonymous; identifiable student data lives only in local browser storage; API calls to Anthropic transmit only course, project, rubric, and aggregate feedback — never personal identifiers.

Privacy Impact & Digital Protection

Before institutional deployment, this tool must undergo a Privacy Impact Assessment and Digital Protection Risk Assessment through ITS, per Carleton's AI Framework. The current prototype is a local-first design intentionally built to minimize PIA surface area.

Pedagogical grounding

AI-assisted assessment is grounded in four pillars: Hattie & Timperley's feedback model (feed up / back / forward); Black & Wiliam on formative assessment; Engeström's expansive learning; Panadero's self-regulated learning review. The instructor always curates before release.
